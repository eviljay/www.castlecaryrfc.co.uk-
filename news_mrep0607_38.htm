Match report: Development Squad
CCRFC v Ivel Barbarians

18/03/2007 		<< News homepage
 
< Mar–Apr 2007

UNDER 7s
Two games were played in sunny but very windy conditions, the pitch having recovered well from a couple of weeks ago. The first game consisted of very open running with tries by Chris Dowding and Dan Edwards. Cary was caught out by the offside rule a few times, but when in possession produced some good passing and support for team tries as well as individual runs.

In the second game, the players showed more concentration and an excellent five-player passing move for a try by Kieran McCaul. Ned also scored. All other tries (6!) by Chris Dowding. Man of the Match went to Ned for staying alert and supporting play throughout.

UNDER 8s
On a very windy day, Ivel and Cary exchanged tries, but in the second half Ivel had turn over ball from which they managed to score the try, and which secured the win for them. Man of the Match went to Matthew Travers for scoring five tries.

UNDER 9s
On a very bright, windy day Cary started very well, battling hard in the rucks and mauls. Passing and running well but eventually in both games overpowered. Haidon Watts ran in both tries for Cary, with good strong tackling from Joe Shepherd and Angus Davis. Haidon Watts was awarded Man of the Match.

UNDER 10s
Cary played well against a well-drilled Ivel team. With support from all over the pitch, Cary put their pace and strength to good use to score the first try by Jack Clothier. George Shepherd scored the second try, with excellent support from Matt. Then Ivel scored to bring the score to 10–5 at half-time. Jack Clothier scored again from the kick-off, along with Tom Wilkinson to bring the score to 20–5. The second game was much the same. Man of the Match went to Charles Proud.

UNDER 11s
On a very windy but bright morning, Cary faced a well-drilled Ivel side. After Cary let Ivel in at the corner for the first try of the game, Joe Hinchcliffe used the wind to kick the ball behind the Ivel line for the first Cary try. Their second came from some good forwards work from Josh Vincent, whose pick and drive sent George Parry over the line. Ivel stole the game at the last minute with a solo effort in the corner. Man of the Match went to Joe Hinchcliffe.

UNDER 12s
Cary showed great commitment and fierce tackling against the County Champions. Richard Johnson and James Clothier scored the three tries; Peter Key converted two of them. Man of the Match went to Richard Johnson.

by Rosie Meek
